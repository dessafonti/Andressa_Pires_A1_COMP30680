Hey there, I'm Andressa, a proud Brazilian woman born and raised in the heart of a big Brazilian city. Since I was a kid, I've had this incredible connection with Brazilian culture, and it all began with my love for literature. When I was just 12 years old, I stumbled upon the mesmerizing writings of Clarice Lispector. Her introspective and thought-provoking stories struck a chord with me, and I couldn't get enough. It was as if she was speaking directly to me, unraveling the mysteries of life, emotions, and Brazil's identity. As I grew older, I eagerly dived into the enchanting worlds crafted by literary giants like Machado de Assis, Carlos Drummond de Andrade, Marina Colassanti, Carolina Maria de Jesus, Manoel de Barros, Caio Fernando Abreu, and many more. Each of these writers painted a unique portrait of Brazil's history, culture, and society

With a master's degree in Sociology, Literature and Education, I've been on a mission to highlight the incredible richness of Brazilian culture to the world. This project is just another example of my dedication to this cause. My journey through Brazilian literature and culture has not only shaped me personally but has also ignited a burning passion to share Brazil's precious heritage with a global audience.

I believe that culture has the power to change lives and bridge gaps between people from different backgrounds. My goal is to inspire others to explore the profound beauty and depth of Brazil's artistic legacy, just as it has transformed my life.
